Privacy Policy

Your privacy and the confidentiality of your information are our highest priorities.

Last Updated: July 10, 2026

ZYA Law Firm is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information in compliance with Canadian privacy laws, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA).

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information necessary to provide legal services, including:

  • Contact Information: Name, address, phone number, email address
  • Identity Information: Date of birth, passport details, citizenship status
  • Immigration Information: Immigration history, status, application details
  • Financial Information: Payment details for legal services
  • Website Usage: IP address, browser type, pages visited (via cookies)

2. How We Use Your Information

Your personal information is used for the following purposes:

  • Providing legal advice and representation
  • Preparing and submitting immigration applications
  • Communicating with immigration authorities on your behalf
  • Billing and payment processing
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Improving our website and services

3. Solicitor-Client Privilege

All communications between you and ZYA Law Firm are protected by solicitor-client privilege. This is a fundamental legal principle that ensures confidentiality in the lawyer-client relationship. We will not disclose privileged communications without your explicit consent, except as required by law.

4. Information Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share information with:

  • Immigration Authorities: As necessary for your immigration applications
  • Service Providers: Trusted third parties who assist in our operations (e.g., translators, document processors)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by court order or applicable law

All third parties are bound by confidentiality obligations.

5. Data Security

We implement robust security measures to protect your personal information:

  • Encrypted data storage and transmission
  • Secure physical and digital file management
  • Access controls and authentication protocols
  • Regular security audits and updates

6. Your Rights

Under Canadian privacy law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Withdraw consent (subject to legal limitations)
  • File a complaint with the Privacy Commissioner of Canada

7. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies to enhance your browsing experience and analyze website traffic. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. We do not use cookies to collect personally identifiable information without your consent.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
